yper Cars – Extremely high-performance cars with advanced technology and prices to match.

Example: Bugatti Chiron, McLaren P1, Ferrari LaFerrari
Vintage Cars – Classic cars that are typically at least 20 years old and often restored or preserved.

Example: 1960s Ford Mustang, 1950s Chevrolet Corvette, 1960 Jaguar E-Type
Most Popular Cars – Cars that have widespread appeal and are commonly seen on the road.

Example: Toyota Corolla, Honda Civic, Ford F-150
Iconic Cars – Cars that are legendary, often due to their design, performance, or cultural significance.

Example: Porsche 911, Ford Mustang, Lamborghini Miura
Luxury Cars – High-end cars focused on comfort, quality, and prestigious brands.

Example: Rolls-Royce Phantom, Bentley Continental GT, Mercedes S-Class
Sports Cars – Cars designed for high-speed and agile handling, usually two-seater.

Example: Chevrolet Corvette, Ferrari 488, Porsche 911
Electric Cars – Cars powered by electric motors instead of internal combustion engines.

Example: Tesla Model S, Nissan Leaf, Rivian R1T
Off-Road Cars – Vehicles built for tough terrains like dirt, rocks, and mud.

Example: Jeep Wrangler, Land Rover Defender, Toyota Land Cruiser
Supercars – High-performance sports cars but not quite at the level of hypercars.

Example: Ferrari 488 GTB, McLaren 720S, Lamborghini Huracán
Convertible Cars – Cars with a roof that can be folded back or removed.

Example: BMW Z4, Mazda MX-5 Miata, Audi R8 Spyder
Family Cars – Practical cars with plenty of room for passengers and luggage.
Example: Honda CR-V, Toyota Highlander, Ford Escape
Concept Cars – Prototype vehicles designed to showcase future designs and technologies.
Example: BMW Vision Next 100, Mercedes-Benz F 015, Lexus LF-LC
